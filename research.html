Research & Publications

My published research and studies focused on HCI, VR accessibility and usability for elderly users. Below are summaries and links to access the full materials.

Adaptive VR Interfaces for Elderly Healthcare Monitoring

Platform: Zenodo — DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.17790985

This paper investigates barriers elderly users experience when interacting with VR-based healthcare applications and proposes interface adaptations that minimize cognitive load, increase readability, and improve user confidence. Methods included user studies, prototype testing, and a set of practical design recommendations for accessible VR dashboards.

WhatsApp Usability Evaluation for Elderly Users

This study consisted of structured usability tests, cognitive walkthroughs, and task-based evaluations with elderly participants to identify accessibility friction points in WhatsApp. Findings include recommendations for larger touch targets, simplified navigation, clearer affordances, and contextual reminders to help users find features more reliably.

The research produced a prioritized list of design changes and low-effort interaction improvements that significantly reduced task completion time in follow-up tests.

Study type: Usability EvaluationOutcome: Interaction redesign recommendations

ElderVR — Adaptive Healthcare Dashboard (Research Summary)

ElderVR is a research prototype that blends VR metaphors with a simplified healthcare dashboard targeted at elderly users. The system emphasizes minimal cognitive load, large readable controls, medicine reminders, stress-relief interactions, and passive monitoring components presented in a calm, high-contrast layout.

Development included multiple iterative user tests, low-fidelity and high-fidelity prototyping, and evaluation metrics focusing on learnability and error rates for common tasks.

Research approach & methodology

My research follows a user-centred approach: problem framing, literature review, participatory design, prototype development, and iterative evaluation. Methods frequently used include usability testing, cognitive walkthroughs, think-aloud protocols, task analysis, statistical analysis of task times & error rates, and qualitative interviews to understand mental models.
